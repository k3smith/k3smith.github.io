Securing the Cyber-Physical Ecosystem of Smart Ports: Resilience in Intermodal Operations and Autonomous Vehicle Integration

Years: 2021 - Present

This research addresses the escalating cybersecurity challenges arising from the modernization of port operations, the increased reliance on intermodal transportation, and the integration of autonomous vehicles (AVs). By focusing on the Port of Virginia as a critical case study, this line of inquiry will develop a novel simulation-based framework to provide a systemic understanding of the vulnerabilities and threats within the complex cyber-physical systems (CPS) ecosystem. This framework will specifically investigate the cybersecurity of operational technology (OT) systems, including vehicle communication networks, and analyze the safety implications of integrating AVs alongside human-driven vehicles. The ultimate goal is to equip port stakeholders with the knowledge and tools necessary to build resilient cybersecurity strategies, ensure the safety and efficiency of port operations, and mitigate the risk of supply chain disruptions in the face of evolving cyber threats.
